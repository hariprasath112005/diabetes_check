How to Check Your Sugar Level

Using a Glucometer

Wash your hands: Clean hands are important to get an accurate reading.

Insert a test strip into the glucometer.

Prick your finger with a lancet to draw a small drop of blood.

Place the blood on the test strip, and wait for the reading.

Read the results on the glucometer screen. This will tell you your current blood glucose level.

Continuous Glucose Monitor (CGM)

A CGM device includes a small sensor placed under the skin, typically on the arm or abdomen.

The sensor measures glucose levels in interstitial fluid every few minutes.

View real-time results on a compatible device, like a smartphone or the CGM reader.

Some CGM devices have an app that sends alerts when your levels are too high or low.

HbA1c Test (Lab Test)

This test is done at a clinic or lab and measures your average blood glucose levels over the past 2-3 months.

It provides an indication of long-term blood sugar control.

Your doctor can order this test, or you can get it done at some pharmacies and testing centers.
